Health Effects of Air Pollution

Understanding the serious impact of polluted air on human health and vulnerable populations

Respiratory System

Asthma: Air pollution triggers asthma attacks and worsens existing conditions, affecting over 300 million people worldwide.

COPD: Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease develops from long-term exposure to particulate matter and toxic gases.

Lung Cancer: PM2.5 particles and toxic compounds increase lung cancer risk by up to 20% in highly polluted areas.

Cardiovascular System

Heart Disease: Fine particles enter bloodstream, causing inflammation and increasing heart attack risk by 24%.

Stroke: Air pollution contributes to 29% of stroke deaths globally through blood clot formation and vessel damage.

Hypertension: Long-term exposure to NO2 and PM2.5 increases blood pressure and cardiovascular mortality.

Neurological Impact

Cognitive Decline: Air pollution accelerates memory loss and increases dementia risk by 40% in seniors.

Brain Development: Children exposed to high pollution show reduced cognitive performance and behavioral issues.

Mental Health: Studies link air pollution to increased rates of depression, anxiety, and suicide.

Most Vulnerable Groups

üë∂ Children & Infants

Children are particularly vulnerable because their lungs are still developing and they breathe more air relative to their body weight.

  • Increased risk of respiratory infections and reduced lung function
  • Higher rates of asthma and allergies in polluted environments
  • Impaired cognitive development and learning disabilities
  • Low birth weight and premature births in polluted areas

üë¥ Elderly Population

Aging immune systems and pre-existing health conditions make seniors more susceptible to pollution-related health problems.

  • Accelerated progression of cardiovascular and respiratory diseases
  • Increased hospitalizations during high pollution episodes
  • Higher mortality rates in areas with poor air quality
  • Faster cognitive decline and increased dementia risk

üè• Pre-existing Conditions

Individuals with chronic health conditions face amplified risks from air pollution exposure.

  • Asthma patients: More frequent and severe attacks
  • Heart disease: Increased risk of heart attacks and arrhythmias
  • Diabetes: Poor blood sugar control and increased complications
  • Pregnant women: Risk of complications and birth defects

Effects by Pollutant Type

PM2.5 (Fine Particles)

Most Dangerous

Particles smaller than 2.5 micrometers penetrate deep into lungs and bloodstream, causing systemic inflammation and organ damage.

NO2 (Nitrogen Dioxide)

Respiratory Irritant

Causes airway inflammation, reduced lung function, and increased susceptibility to respiratory infections.

O3 (Ground-level Ozone)

Lung Irritant

Triggers asthma, reduces lung function, and causes chest pain and coughing, especially during physical activity.

SO2 (Sulfur Dioxide)

Acute Effects

Causes immediate respiratory symptoms, bronchospasm, and can trigger asthma attacks within minutes of exposure.

CO (Carbon Monoxide)

Silent Killer

Reduces oxygen delivery to organs, causing headaches, dizziness, and potentially fatal poisoning at high levels.

PM10 (Coarse Particles)

Upper Respiratory

Irritates eyes, nose, and throat; can worsen asthma and cause coughing and sneezing.
